Tomb 34

Read below to learn about the main grave explored in our project!

Tomb 34 is a cappuccina grave found in Area B at the Gabii excavation site. Cappuccina graves - not to be confused with the popular coffee drink - are made from lead or clay Roman roof tiles arranged in a tent-like structure. Cappuccina graves were common in Imperial Rome and were typically used for lower-class adults.

What makes Tomb 34 so special?

Tomb 34 was composed of two layers. The outside layer of the cappuccina was made from two flat lead sheets also called tegulae and two imbrices (curved roof tiles) that capped each end. Clay was used as a binding agent to hold the imbrices to the tegulae. The inner cappuccina layer was made from six smaller tegulae that interlocked at the top. Tomb 34 was laid in an East-West orientation and was well-preserved unlike many other cappuccina graves which often had broken tegulae.

Quick Facts About Tomb 34

The grave was in an East-West orientation.

The grave was composed of two layers - an inner and an outer cappuccina.

The outer cappuccina was made using two tegulae while the inner cappuccina was made from six smaller tegulae.
